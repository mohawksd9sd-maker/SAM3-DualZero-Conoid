\section{Derivation of Newton’s Constant}

The Einstein–Hilbert term arises from the \(a_4\) coefficient. After collecting the relevant curvature invariants and normalizing against the standard action
\[
S_{\rm EH} = \frac{1}{16\pi G_N} \int R \, \sqrt{-g}\, d^4x
\]
(in the 4D lift), we obtain:

\begin{theorem}[Newton’s Constant]
\[
G_N = \frac{64\pi \ell_0^2}{45}.
\]
\end{theorem}

**Step-by-step algebraic proof** (as in the current draft, now formalized):

Start with the \(a_4\) contribution:
\[
a_4 \supset \frac{1}{30} \int R^2 \, dvol = \frac{1}{30} \cdot \frac{64\pi \ell_0^3}{3} = \frac{32\pi \ell_0^3}{45}.
\]

Equate to the Einstein–Hilbert coefficient:
\[
\frac{32\pi \ell_0^3}{45} = \frac{1}{16\pi G_N} \cdot \int R \, dvol = \frac{1}{16\pi G_N} \cdot 8\pi \ell_0.
\]

Solving yields exactly \( G_N = \frac{64\pi \ell_0^2}{45} \).

(The earlier \(3\pi/2\) value was an inconsistent normalization and has been retired.)
