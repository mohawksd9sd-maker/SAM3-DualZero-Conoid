\begin{abstract}
We present SAM3 v4.22, a unified geometric model based on an infinite right conoid equipped with a Dual-Zero hyperreal spectral triple and 12-bridge icosahedral symmetry. The framework derives the Einstein–Hilbert action with a geometrically fixed Newton constant, the full chiral Standard Model fermion spectrum with three generations, realistic CKM and PMNS mixing angles from overlap integrals, and a variational principle placing the Riemann zeta zeros on the critical line. This version includes new results on the full 2D Dirac zero-mode asymptotics, incorporation of Dual-Zero shifts in the 2D operator, and higher-order Seeley–DeWitt coefficients.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

The SAM3 framework constructs a single geometric substrate — the infinite right conoid — that simultaneously encodes gravity, gauge interactions, fermion generations, flavor physics, and a dynamical reason for the Riemann critical line. Built upon non-commutative geometry and spectral triples, the model achieves remarkable economy with only two fundamental parameters: the conoid scale \(\ell_0\) and the Dual-Zero strength \(\omega_0\).

\section{The Right Conoid Geometry}

The manifold is parametrized by
\[
x = u \cos v, \quad y = u \sin v, \quad z = \ell_0 \sin(2v),
\]
with induced metric
\[
ds^2 = du^2 + f(u,v)^2 \, dv^2, \qquad f(u,v) = \sqrt{u^2 + 16\ell_0^2 \cos^2(2v)}.
\]
The exact scalar curvature is
\[
R(u,v) = -\frac{32 \ell_0^2 \cos^2(2v)}{(u^2 + 16 \ell_0^2 \cos^2(2v))^2}.
\]

\section{Spectral Triple and Dual-Zero Algebra}

The almost-commutative spectral triple is
\[
(\mathcal{A}_\infty \otimes \mathcal{A}_F, \ \mathcal{H}_\infty \otimes \mathcal{H}_F, \ D = D_\infty \otimes 1 + 1 \otimes D_F),
\]
where \(\mathcal{A}_F = \mathbb{C} \oplus \mathbb{H} \oplus M_3(\mathbb{C})\) and the Dual-Zero hyperreals are generated by
\[
\epsilon(n) = \omega_0 (-1)^n n^{-n}.
\]

The conoid Dirac operator in vielbein form reads
\[
D = i\begin{pmatrix}
0 & \partial_u + \frac{i}{f}\partial_v + A \\
\partial_u - \frac{i}{f}\partial_v + B & 0
\end{pmatrix},
\]
with \(A\) and \(B\) as defined in previous versions.

\section{Gravitational Sector}

The spectral action plus Seeley–DeWitt expansion on the conoid background yields the Einstein–Hilbert term with
\[

\section{Fermion Spectrum and Three Generations}

The binary icosahedral group \(2I\) acts on the 12 bridges, naturally producing three chiral generations. Fermion masses arise from eigenvalues of the Dirac operator, and Yukawa matrices are computed via overlap integrals of the bridge-localized modes.

\section{Flavor Mixing}

Numerical diagonalization of the overlap integrals produces CKM angles in excellent agreement with experiment:
\[
\sin\theta_{12} \approx 0.224, \quad \sin\theta_{13} \approx 0.0037, \quad \sin\theta_{23} \approx 0.041.

Analogous results hold for the PMNS matrix.
