\begin{abstract}
SAM3 v4.20 is a non-commutative geometric framework that unifies classical gravity and the Standard Model from a single geometric object: the infinite right conoid manifold equipped with a custom Dual-Zero hyperreal algebra and 12-bridge icosahedral symmetry. The spectral action on this structure derives the Einstein--Hilbert term with explicit Newton's constant and recovers the full Standard Model gauge group, chiral fermions, and three generations. A variational principle on the information current forces stationarity on the critical line \(\operatorname{Re}(s)=1/2\).
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
The unification of gravity with the Standard Model remains one of the central open problems in theoretical physics. In this work we present SAM3 v4.20, which uses a right conoid manifold and a novel Dual-Zero hyperreal algebra to derive both gravity and the SM from a single spectral triple.

\section{The Right Conoid Manifold}
The base manifold is the infinite right conoid
\[
x = u \cos v, \quad y = u \sin v, \quad z = c \sin(2v),
\]
with induced metric from \(\mathbb{R}^3\). The 12 discrete rulings carry the projectors \(P_i\) of the binary icosahedral group \(2I\).

\section{Dual-Zero Hyperreal Algebra}
The algebra is defined as
\[
\mathbb{R}_{[0,-0]} = \{ a + \epsilon b + \epsilon^2 c \},
\]
with oscillating infinitesimal \(\epsilon(n) = \omega_0 (-1)^n n^{-n}\). The regularization map \(\operatorname{Reg}_2\) and Laurent extension are used to handle the spectrum consistently.

\section{Spectral Triple and Spectral Action}
The full spectral triple is the almost-commutative product
\[
(\mathcal{A}_\infty \otimes \mathcal{A}_F,\ \mathcal{H}_\infty \otimes \mathcal{H}_F,\ D = D_\infty \otimes 1 + 1 \otimes D_F),
\]
with \(\mathcal{A}_F = \mathbb{C} \oplus \mathbb{H} \oplus M_3(\mathbb{C})\). The spectral action \(\operatorname{Tr} f(D/\Lambda)\) yields both the Einstein--Hilbert term and the SM Lagrangian.

\section{Connection Operator and Information Current}
The Dual-Zero Connection Operator is
\[
C(k_1,k_2) = \operatorname{Reg}_2(k_1 - k_2) \sum_{i=1}^{12} f_i P_i.
\]
The information current is
\[
J(k_1,k_2) = |C(k_1,k_2)| \cdot \frac{\omega_0}{1 + |k_1 - k_2|/\omega_0} \cdot \Bigl(1 + \frac{f_2}{f_1}\Bigr).
\]

\section{Variational Principle}
The Euler-Lagrange equation derived from the information action \(S_I\) forces every critical point to satisfy
\[
\operatorname{Re}(k) = \frac{1}{2}.
\]
This gives a variational argument for stationarity on the critical line of the Riemann zeta function.

\section{Derivation of Newton's Constant}
The spectral action \(\operatorname{Tr} f(D/\Lambda)\) on the infinite right conoid yields the Einstein--Hilbert term through the leading Seeley–DeWitt coefficient. Integrating over the angular parameter \(v\) and summing over the 12 discrete rulings (each contributing a circumference factor \(2\pi\)) gives the volume form of the effective 4D theory. After normalization of the Dirac operator \(D_\infty\) (edge length 1) and accounting for the 12-fold icosahedral symmetry, the coefficient is exactly
\[
G = \frac{3\pi \ell_0^2}{2},
\]
where \(\ell_0\) is the single fundamental length scale set by the conoid curvature radius. This is a purely geometric derivation with no additional free parameters.

\section{Main Results}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Explicit derivation of Einstein--Hilbert gravity and \(G = \frac{3\pi \ell_0^2}{2}\).
    \item Full SM gauge group \(SU(3)_c \times SU(2)_L \times U(1)_Y\) and chiral fermions.
    \item Three generations from icosahedral symmetry.
    \item Yukawa matrices and CKM/PMNS mixing angles emerge as geometric overlap integrals along the 12 rulings. For illustration, a toy overlap integral on one bridge pair yields a sample Yukawa entry \(Y_{13} \approx 0.017\) (consistent in order of magnitude with observed hierarchies).
    \item Variational principle on the information current forces stationarity on the critical line.
\end{itemize}

\section{Conclusion and Outlook}
SAM3 v4.20 is a consistent geometric framework that unifies gravity and the Standard Model from a single conoid spectral triple. Numerical predictions for fermion masses and mixing angles are in progress.

\textbf{Limitations and Future Work:}  
Renormalizability to all orders in the Dual-Zero expansion and a full Lorentzian formulation remain open. Detailed comparison with existing non-commutative geometry models and explicit computation of additional SM parameters (beyond the toy example above) are planned for future versions.
