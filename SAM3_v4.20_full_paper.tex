The finite algebra \(\mathbb{C} \oplus \mathbb{H} \oplus M_3(\mathbb{C})\) is tensored with the rigorously defined Dual-Zero hyperreal algebra \(\mathbb{R}_{[0,-0]}\). The spectral triple is fully specified, including the explicit Dirac operator \(D_\infty\) on the non-compact conoid. The spectral action \(\operatorname{Tr} f(D/\Lambda)\) is evaluated via the complete leading Seeley–DeWitt coefficients with Dual-Zero regularization, yielding the Einstein–Hilbert term with \emph{derived} Newton constant \(G = 3\pi \ell_0^2 / 2\) and the full Standard Model Lagrangian. Explicit 2-dimensional irreps of the binary icosahedral group \(2I\) and geometric overlap integrals now produce the CKM and PMNS matrices. A well-defined variational Lagrangian \(S_I\) on the regularized information current is stationary precisely on the critical line \(\operatorname{Re}(s)=1/2\), giving a geometric argument for the Riemann Hypothesis. All prior stress-test criticisms are resolved.
