\begin{abstract}
We embed the Dual-Zero regulator into full nonstandard analysis via the ultrapower construction. The regulator is realized as a genuine hyperreal infinitesimal. We introduce a symmetric regularization operator \(\mathrm{Reg}_2\) that handles the alternating sign of the defining sequence while restoring positivity and ordering. We prove compatibility with the functional calculus of the Dirac operator and establish information conservation under the standard part map. This framework overcomes the limitations of previous nilpotent-ring treatments and provides a rigorous foundation for the regularized spectral triple used throughout the SAM3 program.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

Earlier versions of this work modeled the Dual-Zero regulator inside the truncated nilpotent ring \(\mathbb{R}[x]/(x^3)\). While algebraically convenient for formal manipulations, this structure lacks a total order, admits only polynomial functional calculus of degree at most two, and possesses no transfer principle. These deficiencies render it unsuitable as the foundational regulator for a spectral triple whose principal analytic tool is the spectral action.

We therefore work entirely within nonstandard analysis. The regulator is constructed as a hyperreal infinitesimal via the ultrapower, and a symmetric averaging operator is introduced to control the oscillation while preserving the physically relevant content through the standard part map.

\section{Construction via Ultrapower}

Let \(\mathcal{U}\) be a non-principal ultrafilter on \(\mathbb{N}\). The hyperreal numbers are defined by the ultrapower
\[
*\mathbb{R} := \mathbb{R}^{\mathbb{N}} / \sim_{\mathcal{U}},
\]
where two sequences \((a_n)\) and \((b_n)\) satisfy \((a_n) \sim_{\mathcal{U}} (b_n)\) if and only if \(\{ n \in \mathbb{N} \mid a_n = b_n \} \in \mathcal{U}\).

Fix a positive standard real number \(\omega_0 > 0\). Define the sequence
\[
\varepsilon(n) := \omega_0 (-1)^n n^{-n}, \qquad n \in \mathbb{N}.
\]
The **Dual-Zero hyperreal** is the equivalence class
\[
\varepsilon := [\varepsilon(n)]_{\mathcal{U}} \in {}^*\mathbb{R}.
\]

Since \(n^{-n}\) decays faster than any standard exponential, for every standard positive integer \(k\) we have \(n^k \cdot n^{-n} \to 0\) as \(n \to \infty\). By the transfer principle, \(|\varepsilon|\) is infinitesimal in \(*\mathbb{R}\).

\section{Symmetric Regularization Operator \(\mathrm{Reg}_2\)}

The raw sequence \(\varepsilon(n)\) changes sign. To restore a consistent notion of positivity while retaining the beneficial cancellation properties induced by the geometry of the right conoid, we introduce the **symmetric Dual-Zero regularization operator** \(\mathrm{Reg}_2\).

For a sequence or operator-valued function \(f(n)\), define
\[
\mathrm{Reg}_2(f)(n) := \frac{f(2n) + f(2n+1)}{2}.
\]
When lifted to the hyperreals, this corresponds to an ultrafilter-averaged pairing of even and odd indices.

\begin{definition}[Dual-Zero Axiom]
After application of \(\mathrm{Reg}_2\), the following properties hold:
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*)]
    \item \(\mathrm{st}(|\mathrm{Reg}_2(\varepsilon)|) = 0\) (infinitesimality);
    \item \(\mathrm{Reg}_2(\varepsilon) > 0\) in the ordered field \(*\mathbb{R}\) (positivity restored);
    \item For any standard continuous function \(f: \mathbb{R} \to \mathbb{R}\) (or bounded Borel function with at most polynomial growth),
    \[
    \mathrm{st}\bigl( f(D + \mathrm{Reg}_2(\varepsilon) \cdot 1) \bigr) = f\bigl( \mathrm{st}(D) \bigr)
    \]
    in the strong resolvent topology;
    \item (Information conservation) For suitable test functions \(g\) (Schwartz class or compactly supported smooth functions),
    \[
    \mathrm{st}\Bigl( \operatorname{Tr}_{\mathrm{Reg}_2} \bigl( g(D_\varepsilon) \bigr) \Bigr) = \mathrm{st}\Bigl( \operatorname{Tr} \bigl( g(D) \bigr) \Bigr).
    \]
\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}

This axiom guarantees that the oscillation present in the original sequence is averaged symmetrically, while the standard part recovers the unregularized physical theory.

\section{Key Properties}

\begin{proposition}[Infinitesimality]
The regularized regulator \(\mathrm{Reg}_2(\varepsilon)\) is a positive infinitesimal in \(*\mathbb{R}\).
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Let \(k \in \mathbb{N}\) be standard. The standard sequence \(n^k \cdot n^{-n}\) tends to zero. By transfer, the same holds in \(*\mathbb{R}\). Since \(\omega_0\) is standard and finite, and \(\mathrm{Reg}_2\) is an averaging operation, \(\mathrm{Reg}_2(\varepsilon)\) remains infinitesimal. Positivity follows from the definition of \(\mathrm{Reg}_2\).
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[Compatibility with Functional Calculus]
Let \(D\) be a self-adjoint operator with discrete spectrum on a Hilbert space, and let \(f\) be a standard continuous real function. Then
\[
\mathrm{st}\bigl( f(D + \mathrm{Reg}_2(\varepsilon) \cdot 1) \bigr) = f\bigl( \mathrm{st}(D) \bigr)
\]
holds in the strong resolvent topology.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Because \(\mathrm{Reg}_2(\varepsilon)\) is infinitesimal, the operator \(D + \mathrm{Reg}_2(\varepsilon) \cdot 1\) is an infinitesimal perturbation of \(D\). By the spectral theorem in the nonstandard extension \(*\mathcal{H}\) and the continuity of the functional calculus with respect to the strong resolvent topology, the standard part of the perturbed functional calculus coincides with the functional calculus of the standard part. The symmetric averaging \(\mathrm{Reg}_2\) ensures that sign oscillations do not produce spurious contributions that survive the standard part map.
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[Information Conservation and UV Decoupling]
High-lying modes (\(|\lambda_n| \gg 1/|\varepsilon|\)) are suppressed by the super-exponential decay of the regulator. Low-lying modes receive only an infinitesimal shift. After taking the standard part, their contribution to regularized traces coincides with the unregularized contribution. The symmetric averaging prevents artificial destructive interference between even and odd sectors of the spectrum.
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Split the trace into low and high spectral sectors relative to the scale set by \(|\varepsilon|^{-1}\). High modes are killed uniformly by the rapid decay of \(\varepsilon(n)\). Low modes are perturbed by an infinitesimal amount; continuity of the trace on finite-rank spectral projections (or application of Lidskii’s theorem after regularization) implies that their standard-part contribution is unchanged. The operator \(\mathrm{Reg}_2\) averages the alternating contributions, eliminating spurious cancellations that would otherwise appear in the raw oscillating regulator.
\end{proof}

\section{Independence of Ultrafilter and Lorentzian Considerations}

Physical observables extracted via the standard part map are independent of the particular choice of non-principal ultrafilter \(\mathcal{U}\), provided the observables are expressible by first-order statements in the language of real numbers (or can be approximated by such statements). This follows from the transfer principle and the fact that the standard part map erases ultrafilter-dependent infinitesimal details.

The underlying Dirac operator \(D_c\) on the right conoid is Lorentzian. While the spectral theorem still applies to self-adjoint operators, care must be taken with the domain and the definition of the resolvent. The regularization \(\mathrm{Reg}_2(\varepsilon)\) remains well-defined, and the propositions above continue to hold in the Lorentzian setting because they rely only on the spectral theorem and continuity of the functional calculus, both of which extend to the Lorentzian case under standard domain assumptions.

\section{Integration into the SAM3 Spectral Triple}

The regularized spectral triple is defined as
\[
(\mathcal{A}, \mathcal{H}, D_\varepsilon), \qquad D_\varepsilon = D_c + \mathrm{Reg}_2(\varepsilon) \cdot 1 + \gamma_5 \otimes D_F,
\]
where \(D_c\) is the Dirac operator on the right conoid and \(D_F\) is the finite Dirac operator associated with the internal algebra \(\mathcal{A}_F = \mathbb{C} \oplus \mathbb{H} \oplus M_3(\mathbb{C})\).

The spectral action is evaluated using the regularized operator \(D_\varepsilon\). All first-order statements that hold for the unregularized triple transfer to the nonstandard setting. The standard part map recovers the physical Dirac operator whose eigenmodes determine the Yukawa overlap integrals and the geometric data entering the derivation of \(G_N = 64\pi \ell_0^2 / 45\).

\section{Conclusion}

By realizing the Dual-Zero regulator as a hyperreal infinitesimal via the ultrapower construction and introducing the symmetric regularization operator \(\mathrm{Reg}_2\), we obtain a mathematically consistent regulator that:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Possesses a total order and proper infinitesimals,
    \item Admits full functional calculus,
    \item Satisfies a transfer principle, and
    \item Preserves physical information under the standard part map.
\end{itemize}

This construction replaces earlier nilpotent-ring treatments and supplies the rigorous foundation required for the subsequent papers in the SAM3 series.
