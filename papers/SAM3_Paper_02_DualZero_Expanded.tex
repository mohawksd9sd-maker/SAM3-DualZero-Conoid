\begin{abstract}
We develop the \textbf{Dual-Zero Regularization Ring} \(\mathbb{R}_{[0,-0]}\), the algebraic foundation of regularization in the SAM3 framework. We give a precise axiomatic definition, prove that it forms a consistent commutative ring, and establish quantitative compatibility with spectral triples, including explicit control on Seeley–DeWitt corrections and positivity bounds. We provide geometric motivation for the regulator sequence tied to the eigenmodes of the right conoid and demonstrate that the construction preserves key physical properties. The framework is compared with standard regularization methods in quantum field theory and non-commutative geometry. This paper supplies the rigorous foundation used throughout the SAM3 series.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

A central technical challenge in spectral approaches to unification is the construction of a regularization scheme that is strong enough to control ultraviolet divergences while remaining fully compatible with the functional calculus of the Dirac operator and preserving the physical content of the theory in the continuum limit.

In the SAM3 framework, which derives the Standard Model and gravity from a Dual-Zero hyperreal spectral triple on the right conoid with icosahedral symmetry, a custom regularization structure is required. This structure must interact cleanly with the discrete 12-bridge regularization and the representation theory of the binary icosahedral group \(2I\).

We introduce the \textbf{Dual-Zero Regularization Ring} \(\mathbb{R}_{[0,-0]}\). This is a truncated nilpotent ring extension of \(\mathbb{R}\) equipped with a geometrically motivated regulator sequence. It is \emph{not} a hyperreal number system in the sense of Robinson; we therefore adopt the precise name \textbf{Dual-Zero Regularization Ring}.

The two suppression mechanisms encoded in the name are:
- Super-exponential decay, which forces rapid truncation of ultraviolet modes.
- Systematic sign alternation, which reflects the geometric oscillation present in the right conoid.

This paper provides the complete algebraic and spectral foundation for the regulator used in all subsequent SAM3 papers.

\section{Definition and Geometric Motivation}

\begin{Definition}[Dual-Zero Regularization Ring]
Fix a positive real scale \(\omega_0 > 0\). The \textbf{Dual-Zero Regularization Ring} is the commutative ring
\[
\mathbb{R}_{[0,-0]} := \mathbb{R}[x] / (x^3),
\]
whose elements are uniquely represented as \(a + b\varepsilon + c\varepsilon^2\) with \(a,b,c \in \mathbb{R}\). Multiplication is the usual polynomial multiplication reduced modulo \(\varepsilon^3 = 0\).

The ring is equipped with the \textbf{regulator sequence}
\[
\varepsilon(n) := \omega_0 (-1)^n n^{-n} \in \mathbb{R}_{[0,-0]}.
\]
\end{Definition}

\subsection{Geometric Motivation for the Regulator Sequence}

On the right conoid, the Dirac eigenmodes can be ordered by increasing eigenvalue magnitude along the rulings. The height function contains the factor \(\sin(2v)\), which changes sign under the natural pairing of modes. When these modes are projected onto the 12-bridge projectors associated with the binary icosahedral symmetry, the projections acquire an alternating character between even and odd modes in the spectral ordering.

The factor \((-1)^n\) is the discrete counterpart of this geometric sign oscillation. The super-exponential decay \(n^{-n}\) is chosen because it dominates any power-law growth and guarantees that the nilpotent truncation \(\varepsilon^3 = 0\) removes ultraviolet contributions without affecting the finite, physically relevant spectrum extracted from the low-lying modes. In the continuum limit \(\omega_0 \to 0\), the regulator reproduces the standard unregularized spectral action.

\section{Algebraic Structure}

\begin{Theorem}
\(\mathbb{R}_{[0,-0]}\) is a commutative local Artinian ring with maximal ideal \((\varepsilon)\) and nilpotency index 3.
\end{Theorem}

\begin{proof}
It is the quotient of the commutative polynomial ring \(\mathbb{R}[x]\) by the principal ideal \((x^3)\). All ring axioms are inherited from \(\mathbb{R}[x]\). The unique prime ideal is \((\varepsilon)\), and the ring has finite length 3 as a module over itself.
\end{proof}

\subsection{Why Nilpotency Index 3 Is Natural}

The spectral action and the information current appearing in the SAM3 variational principle involve up to second-order variations (heat-kernel coefficients up to curvature-squared terms). Truncation at \(\varepsilon^2\) captures all physically relevant corrections while automatically annihilating higher-order terms. This is the minimal index that closes the algebra under the operations required by the SAM3 constructions while preserving the Noetherian and finite-length properties that guarantee controlled truncation of all infinite series arising in regularization.

\begin{Corollary}[Controlled Laurent Extension]
Adjoining formal inverses \(\omega = 1/\varepsilon\) and \(\Omega = 1/\varepsilon^2\) yields a well-defined extension in which every element has only finitely many negative powers. This extension is useful for formal bookkeeping but is never required for the final physical quantities, which remain in the original ring after projection onto the constant term.
\end{Corollary}

\section{Interaction with Spectral Triples}

Let \((\mathcal{A}, \mathcal{H}, D)\) be a spectral triple with discrete spectrum \(\{\lambda_n\}\) and orthonormal eigenbasis \(\{e_n\}\).

\begin{Theorem}[Commutation with Functional Calculus]
For any bounded Borel function \(f\), multiplication by the scalar sequence \(\varepsilon(n)\) commutes with \(f(D)\) in the spectral representation. Consequently, the regularized trace
\[
\operatorname{Tr}_\varepsilon\bigl(f(D)\bigr) := \sum_n f(\lambda_n) \cdot \varepsilon(n)
\]
is well-defined and independent of the choice of eigenbasis.
\end{Theorem}

\begin{Theorem}[Quantitative Seeley–DeWitt Compatibility]
Let \(K(t) = \operatorname{Tr}(e^{-t D^2})\) be the unregularized heat trace, which admits the asymptotic expansion
\[
K(t) \sim \sum_{k=0}^\infty a_k t^{(k-2)/2}, \qquad t \to 0^+,
\]
with remainder \(R_N(t)\) controlled by standard parametrix estimates. Then
\[
\bigl| \operatorname{Tr}_\varepsilon(e^{-t D^2}) - \sum_{k=0}^N a_k t^{(k-2)/2} \bigr| \leq C_N t^{-M} \sum_{n > N_0} n^{-n} + |R_N(t)|.
\]
The tail of the regulator is bounded by \(\exp(-c N_0^\alpha)\) for any \(\alpha < 1\). Thus the difference between the regularized and classical expansions is exponentially small.
\end{Theorem}

\begin{Theorem}[Quantitative Positivity]
Let \(f\) be a cutoff function that is positive on \([0, \infty)\) and decays faster than any exponential. Write the regularized spectral action as
\[
S_\varepsilon = S_0 + \varepsilon S_1 + \varepsilon^2 S_2.
\]
Then \(S_0 > 0\) and
\[
|S_1| + |S_2| \leq C \sum_n |f(\lambda_n)| \cdot n^{-n} \leq C' \exp(-c N_0^\alpha)
\]
for sufficiently large spectral cutoff. The non-Archimedean corrections are therefore quantitatively negligible.
\end{Theorem}

\section{Preservation of Physical Properties}

\begin{Theorem}[Diffeomorphism Covariance in the Limit]
The regulator is a scalar sequence applied after the spectral decomposition of the covariant operator \(D\). Under a diffeomorphism, \(D\) transforms covariantly and the spectrum is preserved up to unitary equivalence. Therefore the Archimedean (constant) term of the regularized action transforms exactly as the classical spectral action.
\end{Theorem}

\begin{Theorem}[Gauge Invariance of the Finite Part]
The finite non-commutative algebra \(\mathcal{A}_F\) and its associated Dirac operator are completely unaffected by the regulator. Gauge invariance of the finite spectral action is therefore preserved exactly.
\end{Theorem}

\begin{Theorem}[Absence of Spurious Violations]
Because the regulator sequence is real-valued and commutes with the functional calculus, it introduces no imaginary parts and does not generate violations of Ward identities or unitarity that survive projection onto the physical (constant) term.
\end{Theorem}
