\begin{abstract}
We embed the Dual-Zero regulator into rigorous nonstandard analysis via the ultrapower construction. The regulator is realized as a genuine hyperreal infinitesimal. We introduce the symmetric regularization operator \(\mathrm{Reg}_2\) that restores positivity while preserving information. We prove compatibility with the functional calculus, information conservation under the standard-part map, Krein-space stability, and ultrafilter independence. Scheme-dependence comparisons with conventional cutoffs are provided. This construction supplies the rigorous ultraviolet regulator used throughout the SAM3-DualZero-Conoid program.

\section{Introduction}

Earlier versions of the SAM3 framework employed a truncated nilpotent ring \(\mathbb{R}[x]/(x^3)\). While convenient for formal manipulations, this structure lacks a total order, full functional calculus, and a transfer principle. We therefore adopt the ultrapower construction of nonstandard analysis, which resolves all these deficiencies and provides a mathematically sound foundation for the regularized spectral triple on the right conoid.

\section{Ultrapower Construction of the Dual-Zero Regulator}

Let \(\mathcal{U}\) be a non-principal ultrafilter on \(\mathbb{N}\). The hyperreal field is the ultrapower
\[
{}^*\mathbb{R} = \mathbb{R}^{\mathbb{N}} / \sim_{\mathcal{U}}.
\]
Fix \(\omega_0 \approx 0.97 \in \mathbb{R}^+\). Define the sequence
\[
\varepsilon(n) := \omega_0 (-1)^n n^{-n}, \qquad n \in \mathbb{N}.
\]
The **Dual-Zero hyperreal** is the equivalence class
\[
\varepsilon := [\varepsilon(n)]_{\mathcal{U}} \in {}^*\mathbb{R}.
\]
Super-exponential decay implies that \(\varepsilon\) is infinitesimal: for every standard \(k \in \mathbb{N}\), \(|\varepsilon| < 1/k\) in \({}^*\mathbb{R}\).

\section{Symmetric Regularization Operator \(\mathrm{Reg}_2\)}

To restore positivity while preserving the beneficial oscillation-induced cancellations on the right conoid, we define
\[
\mathrm{Reg}_2(f)(n) := \frac{f(2n) + f(2n+1)}{2}.
\]
The regularized infinitesimal is \(\eta := \mathrm{Reg}_2(\varepsilon)\). After lifting to the hyperreals, \(\eta > 0\) and still infinitesimal.

\begin{definition}[Dual-Zero Axiom]

\begin{enumerate}[label=(\roman*)]

    \item Functional calculus compatibility: \(\mathrm{st}\bigl(f(D_c + \eta \cdot 1)\bigr) = f(\mathrm{st}(D_c))\) in the strong resolvent topology for suitable \(f\);

\end{enumerate}
\end{definition}
