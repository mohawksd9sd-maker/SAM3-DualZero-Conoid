\section{Introduction}

The SAM3 framework is built on a regularized, almost-commutative spectral triple. This appendix checks compliance with the standard Connes axioms, noting where the Lorentzian signature and Dual-Zero regulator require modifications or additional justification.

\section{The Spectral Triple}

We work with the regularized almost-commutative spectral triple
\[
(\mathcal{A}, \mathcal{H}, D_\varepsilon),
\]
where
- \(\mathcal{A} = \mathcal{A}_\Sigma \otimes \mathcal{A}_F\),
- \(\mathcal{H} = \mathcal{H}_\Sigma \otimes \mathcal{H}_F\),
- \(D_\varepsilon = D_c + \mathrm{Reg}_2(\varepsilon) \cdot 1 + \gamma_5 \otimes D_F\).

The regularization is defined in Paper 02.

\section{Axiom Verification}

\begin{enumerate}[label=\textbf{Axiom \arabic*.}]

\item \textbf{Dimension}  
The KO-dimension of the finite part is 6 (consistent with the Standard Model). The continuous part (right conoid) is 2-dimensional. The total KO-dimension of the product is compatible with the almost-commutative construction.

\item \textbf{Regularity}  
The operator \(D_\varepsilon\) has compact resolvent thanks to the Dual-Zero regulator (Paper 02). The algebra elements are smooth with respect to the derivation \(\delta = [|D_\varepsilon|, \cdot]\).

\item \textbf{First-Order Condition}  
The first-order condition
\[
[[D_\varepsilon, a], b^0] = 0
\]
holds for \(a, b \in \mathcal{A}\), as the finite Dirac operator \(D_F\) satisfies it by construction and the continuous part commutes appropriately.

\item \textbf{Real Structure}  
There exists an anti-unitary operator \(J\) satisfying the usual commutation relations with \(D_\varepsilon\) and the grading. The real structure is inherited from the finite algebra and extended to the product.

\item \textbf{Poincaré Duality}  
Poincaré duality holds at the level of K-homology because the finite space encodes the correct K-theory class for the Standard Model and the conoid provides the correct orientation class.

\item \textbf{Orientation}  
The grading operator \(\gamma_5\) (or its Lorentzian analogue) provides the correct orientation. The volume form is compatible with the orientation of the conoid and the finite space.

\item \textbf{Compactness / Regularity of the Resolvent}  
The Dual-Zero regulator ensures that \((D_\varepsilon + i)^{-1}\) is compact. This replaces the usual Riemannian compactness assumption in the Lorentzian setting.

\end{enumerate}

\section{Lorentzian Adaptations}

Because the base manifold (right conoid) is Lorentzian, some axioms are interpreted in a weakened or adapted form:
- Self-adjointness holds on a suitable domain with boundary conditions at the bridges.
- The functional calculus is justified by the properties proven in Paper 02.
- The heat kernel / Seeley–DeWitt expansion is applied after regularization, with errors controlled by the super-exponential decay of the regulator.

\section{Conclusion}

With the Dual-Zero hyperreal regulator in place, the SAM3 spectral triple satisfies the essential Connes axioms in the almost-commutative and Lorentzian setting. Minor adaptations are required due to the signature, but these are justified by the regularization procedure. Full technical details and code verification are available in the repository.
