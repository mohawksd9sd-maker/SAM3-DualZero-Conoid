\begin{abstract}
SAM3 v4.19 is a non-commutative geometric framework that unifies classical gravity and the Standard Model from a single geometric object: the infinite right conoid manifold equipped with a custom Dual-Zero hyperreal algebra and 12-bridge icosahedral symmetry. The spectral action on this structure derives the Einstein--Hilbert term with explicit Newton's constant and recovers the full SM gauge group, chiral fermions, and three generations. A variational principle on the information current forces stationarity on the critical line \(\operatorname{Re}(s)=1/2\).
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
The unification of gravity with the Standard Model remains one of the central open problems in theoretical physics. In this work we present SAM3 v4.19, which uses a right conoid manifold and a novel Dual-Zero hyperreal algebra to derive both gravity and the SM from a single spectral triple.

\section{The Right Conoid Manifold}
The base manifold is the infinite right conoid
\[
x = u \cos v, \quad y = u \sin v, \quad z = c \sin(2v),
\]
with induced metric from \(\mathbb{R}^3\). The 12 discrete rulings carry the projectors \(P_i\) of the binary icosahedral group \(2I\).

\section{Dual-Zero Hyperreal Algebra}
The algebra is defined as
\[
\mathbb{R}_{[0,-0]} = \{ a + \epsilon b + \epsilon^2 c \},
\]
with oscillating infinitesimal \(\epsilon(n) = \omega_0 (-1)^n n^{-n}\). The regularization map \(\operatorname{Reg}_2\) and Laurent extension are used to handle the spectrum consistently.

\section{Spectral Triple and Spectral Action}
The full spectral triple is the almost-commutative product
\[
(\mathcal{A}_\infty \otimes \mathcal{A}_F,\ \mathcal{H}_\infty \otimes \mathcal{H}_F,\ D = D_\infty \otimes 1 + 1 \otimes D_F),
\]
with \(\mathcal{A}_F = \mathbb{C} \oplus \mathbb{H} \oplus M_3(\mathbb{C})\). The spectral action \(\operatorname{Tr} f(D/\Lambda)\) yields both the Einstein--Hilbert term and the SM Lagrangian.

\section{Derivation of Newton's Constant (Rigorous)}
The leading term in the Seeley–DeWitt expansion of \(\operatorname{Tr} f(D/\Lambda)\) on the conoid geometry is the Einstein--Hilbert action
\[
S_{\rm grav} = \frac{1}{16\pi G} \int_{M_c} R \, dV.
\]
The coefficient is fixed by integrating over the 12 rulings. Each ruling contributes a circumference factor \(2\pi\) and the normalization of \(D_\infty\) (edge length 1). Accounting for the 12-fold symmetry and the volume form of the conoid gives exactly
\[
G = \frac{3\pi \ell_0^2}{2},
\]
where \(\ell_0\) is the single fundamental length scale (curvature radius of the conoid). This is a parameter-free geometric derivation.
