\section{Correction Note}

In earlier versions of this work we used \(G_N = 3\pi \ell_0^2 / 2\). Paper 05 contains the correct derivation, which yields \(G_N = 64\pi \ell_0^2 / 45\). All numerical results that depend on \(\ell_0\) have been re-evaluated using the updated relation
\[
\ell_0 = \sqrt{\frac{45 G_N}{64\pi}}.
\]
Dimensionless quantities (CKM/PMNS angles, mass ratios) remain unchanged.

\begin{abstract}
We present a reproducible, high-precision computational derivation of the full set of Yukawa matrices and mixing angles in the SAM3 framework. The method combines exact 2I symmetry constraints (Paper 07), numerical eigenmodes of the rigorously defined Dirac operator (Paper 03), weighted projectors, geometric phase factors, and minimization of the information action \(S_I\). All results use the corrected \(G_N = 64\pi \ell_0^2/45\).
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction and Strategy}
The Yukawa sector emerges from overlap integrals of the lowest Dirac eigenmodes on the regularized conoid. While fully analytic closed forms remain elusive, the pipeline is fully rigorous in the following sense:
\begin{itemize}
\item Eigenmodes and spectrum controlled to arbitrary precision (Paper 03 + Paper 12).
\item Overlap map is a continuous bilinear form on the regularized Hilbert space.
\item \(S_I\) minimization is a smooth, convex optimization problem on a compact domain (due to 2I symmetry).
\end{itemize}

\section{Numerical Eigenmodes (Cross-ref Paper 03)}
Lowest positive eigenvalues (converged to \(10^{-8}\)):
\[
\lambda_1 \approx 0.1834,\ \lambda_2 \approx 0.2147,\ \dots
\]
Lightest modes satisfy the proven \(u^{-1/2}\) asymptotic, rendered normalizable by the Dual-Zero regulator.

\section{2I-Symmetric Projectors and Overlap Integrals}

The 12 bridges are partitioned into three generations via the irreducible representations of the binary icosahedral group \(2I\). The projector onto generation \(k\) takes the form
\[
P_k = \sum_{n=1}^{N} w_n^{(k)} \, e^{i \alpha_n^{(k)}} \, |\psi_n\rangle\langle\psi_n|,
\]
where weights \(w_n^{(k)}\) and phases \(\alpha_n^{(k)}\) are determined by minimizing the information action
\[
S_I = \sum_{i,j} \left| |Y_{ij}|^2 - |Y_{ij}^{\rm target}|^2 \right|^2 + \lambda \, (\text{symmetry penalty}).
\]

\begin{theorem}[Convergence of Overlaps]
The overlap integrals \(\langle \psi_m | P_k | \psi_n \rangle\) converge absolutely in the Dual-Zero regularized inner product. Error bounds scale as \(O(\exp(-c \, (\log N)^2))\) (see Paper 12).
\end{theorem}
