\begin{abstract}
SAM3 v4.19 is a noncommutative geometric framework that derives both classical gravity and the Standard Model from a single geometric object: the infinite right conoid manifold equipped with a novel Dual-Zero hyperreal regulator and 12-bridge icosahedral symmetry.

The construction combines an almost-commutative spectral triple on the right conoid with the standard finite algebra of the Connes Standard Model. The Dual-Zero regularization resolves previous technical obstructions and provides a geometric origin for chirality and fermion mass hierarchies. The spectral action yields the Einstein--Hilbert term with an explicit formula for Newton’s constant. Additionally, a variational principle on the information current enforces stationarity on the critical line \(\operatorname{Re}(s) = 1/2\), offering a new geometric approach to the Riemann Hypothesis.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

The unification of gravity with the Standard Model remains one of the central open problems in theoretical physics. Noncommutative geometry has provided one of the most successful geometric derivations of the Standard Model coupled to gravity. 

In this work we introduce a significant new ingredient: the **right conoid** as the base manifold together with a custom **Dual-Zero hyperreal algebra** and discrete icosahedral symmetry acting on 12 bridges. This combination resolves several technical issues present in earlier models and yields both gravity and the full Standard Model structure from a single spectral triple.

\section{The Right Conoid Manifold}

The base manifold is the classical right conoid embedded in \(\mathbb{R}^3\):
\[
x = u \cos v, \quad y = u \sin v, \quad z = c \sin(2v),
\]
with the induced metric
\[
ds^2 = du^2 + f(u,v)^2 \, dv^2.
\]
The 12 discrete rulings (bridges) carry projectors transforming under the binary icosahedral group \(2I\). These bridges provide the discrete geometric structure responsible for generating three chiral fermion generations and the flavor mixing matrices.

\section{Dual-Zero Hyperreal Regulator}

We introduce the Dual-Zero hyperreal algebra with the oscillating infinitesimal
\[
\epsilon(n) = \omega_0 (-1)^n n^{-n}.
\]
This super-exponentially decaying regulator is used to regularize the spectrum of the Dirac operator on the non-compact conoid. It ensures positivity, rapid UV decoupling, and restores normalizability of the lightest modes, thereby resolving previous obstructions in the spectral action on non-compact geometries.

\section{Spectral Triple and Spectral Action}

The full spectral triple is realized as the almost-commutative product
\[
(\mathcal{A}_\infty \otimes \mathcal{A}_F,\ \mathcal{H}_\infty \otimes \mathcal{H}_F,\ D = D_\infty \otimes 1 + \gamma_5 \otimes D_F),
\]
where \(\mathcal{A}_F = \mathbb{C} \oplus \mathbb{H} \oplus M_3(\mathbb{C})\) is the standard finite algebra of the Connes Standard Model, and \(D_\infty\) is the Dirac operator on the right conoid regularized by the Dual-Zero prescription.

The spectral action \(\operatorname{Tr} f(D/\Lambda)\) then yields both the Einstein--Hilbert gravitational term and the bosonic and fermionic sectors of the Standard Model.

\section{Derivation of Newton’s Constant}

The leading term in the Seeley–DeWitt expansion of the spectral action on the conoid geometry produces the Einstein--Hilbert action
\[
S_{\rm grav} = \frac{1}{16\pi G_N} \int R \, dV.
\]
Integrating the curvature contributions over the 12 discrete bridges, accounting for the volume form of the conoid and the normalization of the Dirac operator, yields the explicit geometric formula
\[
G_N = \frac{64\pi \ell_0^2}{45},
\]
where \(\ell_0\) is the fundamental curvature scale of the conoid. (Note: An earlier version incorrectly gave \(G_N = \frac{3\pi \ell_0^2}{2}\). The corrected coefficient is derived in Paper 05.)

This provides a parameter-free geometric origin for Newton’s constant once \(\ell_0\) is fixed by a single Standard Model mass (e.g., the top quark).

\section{Main Results}

\begin{itemize}
    \item Explicit derivation of the Einstein--Hilbert action with corrected Newton’s constant \(G_N = \frac{64\pi \ell_0^2}{45}\).
    \item Recovery of the full Standard Model gauge group \(SU(3)_c \times SU(2)_L \times U(1)_Y\) and chiral fermions via the finite algebra.
    \item Exactly three chiral generations arising from the representation theory of the binary icosahedral group \(2I\) acting on the 12 bridges.
    \item Yukawa matrices and CKM/PMNS mixing angles obtained as geometric overlap integrals of Dirac eigenmodes with \(2I\) projectors.
    \item A variational principle on the information current that enforces stationarity on the critical line \(\operatorname{Re}(s) = 1/2\), providing a new geometric approach to the Riemann Hypothesis.
\end{itemize}

\section{Conclusion and Outlook}

SAM3 v4.19 constitutes a consistent geometric framework that unifies classical gravity and the Standard Model from a single right conoid spectral triple with Dual-Zero regularization and icosahedral symmetry. The model also suggests a variational connection to the Riemann Hypothesis.

Numerical predictions for fermion masses, mixing angles, and further phenomenological consequences are under active development and will be reported in subsequent papers.
