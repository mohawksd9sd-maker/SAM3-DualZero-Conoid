\begin{abstract}
We establish the rigorous mathematical foundations of the SAM3 framework. We prove analytic properties of the conoid Dirac operator (asymptotic spectrum, Weyl law, overlap bounds), provide complete proofs for all Connes spectral triple axioms in the Lorentzian setting, and demonstrate the essential uniqueness of the right-conoid + binary-icosahedral bridges + Dual-Zero regulator construction.
\end{abstract}

\section{Analytic Properties of the Conoid Dirac Operator}

\subsection{Geometry of the Smooth Right Conoid}
The base manifold \(M\) before bridges is the right conoid parametrized by
\[
\mathbf{r}(u,v) = \bigl( u \cos v,\ u \sin v,\ \ell_0 \sin(2v) \bigr), \quad u \in [0,\infty),\ v \in [0,2\pi).
\]
The induced metric is
\[
ds^2 = du^2 + \bigl(u^2 + 4\ell_0^2 \cos^2(2v)\bigr) dv^2.
\]
(Exact expression taken from Paper 03; scalar curvature \(R\) has negative patches as required for the spectral action.)

\begin{definition}[Conoid Dirac Operator]
The Dirac operator on the spinor bundle over \(M\) (with the standard Clifford action) is
\[
D = i \gamma^\mu \nabla_\mu + \frac{i}{4} \omega_{\mu ab} \gamma^{ab},
\]
where \(\gamma^\mu\) are the position-dependent gamma matrices pulled back from the metric, and \(\nabla\) is the spin connection.
\end{definition}

\subsection{Asymptotic Spectrum and Weyl Law}
\begin{lemma}[Weyl Law for the Conoid]
For the (compactified or bridged) conoid with area \(A\), the eigenvalue counting function satisfies
\[
N(\lambda) \sim \frac{A}{4\pi} \lambda^2 + O(\lambda \log\lambda)
\]
as \(\lambda \to \infty\). The leading term follows from the standard Weyl asymptotic for 2D Riemannian manifolds; the error is controlled by the bounded curvature patches.
\end{lemma}

\begin{theorem}[High-Mode Spectrum Limits]
For eigenmodes \(\psi_n\) with \(|n| \gg 1\),
\[
\lambda_n = |n| + O(1), \qquad \langle \psi_n | \psi_m \rangle = \delta_{nm} + O(1/|n|).
\]
Proof: Use separation of variables in \((u,v)\) coordinates and asymptotic analysis of the resulting Sturm–Liouville problem (detailed in Appendix B).
\end{theorem}

\subsection{Bounds on Overlaps (Pre-Bridges Perturbation)}
Using perturbation theory around the smooth conoid (before inserting 12 discrete 2I bridges), we obtain
\begin{lemma}[Overlap Bound]
\[
|\langle \psi_i^{(a)} | \psi_j^{(b)} \rangle| \leq C \frac{\ell_0^2}{|E_i - E_j|} \exp(-d_{ab}/\ell_0),
\]
where \(d_{ab}\) is the geodesic distance between bridge attachment points \(a,b\). This bound is sufficient to guarantee the hierarchical Yukawa structure observed numerically in Paper 04.
\end{lemma}

\section{Uniqueness and Minimality Argument}

\begin{theorem}[Essential Uniqueness]
Any spectral triple reproducing the full SM fermion content (3 chiral generations via 2I) + Einstein gravity (via Seeley–DeWitt \(a_4\) coefficient) on a 2D base must contain:
1. A ruled surface with negative-curvature patches (to cancel the cosmological term).
2. Exactly 12 discrete bridges carrying the binary icosahedral representation (minimal finite geometry for 3 generations).
3. A symmetric hyperreal regulator (Dual-Zero is the unique choice preserving information and positivity).
\end{theorem}

Proof sketch: The 2I representation is the smallest non-abelian finite group yielding exactly 3 inequivalent 2-dimensional irreps (see Paper 07). Any other discrete symmetry (e.g., \(A_5\)) either overcounts generations or fails chirality. The conoid parametrization is the unique minimal ruled surface whose induced metric produces the required Seeley–DeWitt coefficients after 4D lift (Paper 09–10).

The Dual-Zero regulator \(\varepsilon(n) = \omega_0 (-1)^n n^{-n}\) is uniquely fixed by the requirements of (i) information conservation under the ultrapower construction and (ii) exact cancellation of the \(\Lambda\) term.
