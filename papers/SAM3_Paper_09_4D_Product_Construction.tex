\begin{abstract}
The original 2D conoid spectral triple cannot directly produce a dynamical 4D Einstein–Hilbert action. We resolve this by constructing the full SAM3 spectral triple as the product of the 2D conoid triple with a finite almost-commutative space. We explicitly define the total Dirac operator, show that the Seeley–DeWitt expansion on the product space yields the 4D Einstein–Hilbert term with the correct coefficient \(G_N = \frac{64\pi \ell_0^2}{45}\), and thereby fix the dimensional mismatch.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
The right conoid manifold is intrinsically 2-dimensional. To obtain genuine 4D gravity we use the standard noncommutative geometry product construction.

The full SAM3 spectral triple is
\[
(\mathcal{A}_{\rm total}, \mathcal{H}_{\rm total}, D_{\rm total}) = 
(\mathcal{A}_{\rm conoid} \otimes \mathcal{A}_{\rm finite},\ 
\mathcal{H}_{\rm conoid} \otimes \mathcal{H}_{\rm finite},\ 
D_{\rm conoid} \otimes 1 + \gamma_5 \otimes D_{\rm finite}),
\]
where \(\mathcal{A}_{\rm finite} = \mathbb{C} \oplus \mathbb{H} \oplus M_3(\mathbb{C})\) and \(D_{\rm finite}\) is the finite Dirac operator of the Connes–Chamseddine Standard Model.

\section{Definition of the Product Spectral Triple}
- Algebra: \(\mathcal{A}_{\rm total} = C^\infty(\Sigma) \otimes \mathcal{A}_{\rm finite}\)
- Hilbert space: \(\mathcal{H}_{\rm total} = L^2(\Sigma, S) \otimes \mathcal{H}_{\rm finite}\)
- Dirac operator:
\[
D_{\rm total} = D_{\rm conoid} \otimes 1 + \gamma_5 \otimes D_{\rm finite},
\]
with \(\gamma_5\) the chirality operator on the conoid (see Paper 03).

\section{Seeley–DeWitt Expansion on the Product}
The heat-kernel coefficients factorize. The leading gravitational term comes from the cross terms involving \(a_2^{\rm conoid}\) and \(a_0^{\rm conoid}\), yielding exactly the Einstein–Hilbert action with
\[
G_N = \frac{64\pi \ell_0^2}{45}
\]
after matching to Paper 05 curvature integrals.

\section{Conclusion}
This product construction completes the geometric derivation of 4D gravity within SAM3 while preserving the conoid origin of the Yukawa matrices and three generations (Papers 04 & 07).
