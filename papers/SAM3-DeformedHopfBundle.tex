\begin{abstract}
We construct the right conoid geometry underlying the SAM3 Dual-Zero spectral triple as the image of the canonical zero section of a \textbf{deformed principal \(S^1\)-bundle} over the cylindrical base \(B = \mathbb{R}_u \times S^1_v\). The deformation of the Hopf connection is chosen to be minimal, \(2I\)-equivariant, and to recover the exact SAM3 induced metric after an explicit global reparametrization. The 12 binary-icosahedral bridges arise canonically as orbits under the lifted \(2I\)-action. This bundle-theoretic formulation unifies the continuous conoid geometry (source of gravity) with the discrete structure (source of three chiral generations) and lifts naturally to the full Lorentzian almost-commutative spectral triple. The construction is shown to be minimal within the class of equivariant first-harmonic deformations and provides a rigorous geometric origin for the Dirac operator separability used in the SAM3 derivations.

\textbf{Keywords:} Noncommutative geometry, spectral triples, Hopf fibration, binary icosahedral group, unification, Lorentzian spectral geometry.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

The SAM3 framework derives both Einstein gravity and the full Standard Model from a single low-dimensional spectral triple built on a right conoid surface equipped with 12 discrete bridges realizing the binary icosahedral group \(2I\). To strengthen the geometric foundations and remove any appearance of ad-hoc construction, we realize the conoid as the zero section of a \textbf{deformed Hopf bundle}. This formulation:

\begin{itemize}
    \item Provides an explicit principal-bundle origin for the metric,
    \item Makes the \(2I\)-symmetry and the 12 bridges canonical,
    \item Facilitates the lift to the Lorentzian spectral triple,
    \item Connects SAM3 to classical Hopf geometry and existing deformed-bundle literature in noncommutative geometry.
\end{itemize}

\section{The Deformed Hopf Bundle}

Let the \textbf{base space} be the cylinder
\[
B = \mathbb{R}_u \times S^1_v
\]
with the flat metric \(du^2 + dv^2\), where \(v \sim v + 2\pi\).

The \textbf{total space} is the trivial principal \(S^1\)-bundle
\[
P = B \times S^1_\theta, \quad \theta \in [0, 2\pi).
\]

Equip \(P\) with the deformed connection 1-form
\[
A = d\theta + \alpha(u,v)\, dv, \qquad \alpha(u,v) = \frac{u}{2} + 4\ell_0 \cos(2v),
\]
where \(\ell_0 > 0\) is the fundamental length scale of SAM3 (anchored to the top-quark mass).

The Riemannian metric on \(P\) is
\[
g_P = du^2 + dv^2 + (d\theta + \alpha(u,v)\, dv)^2.
\]

\section{Canonical Section and Global Reparametrization}

Consider the zero section
\[
\sigma_0: B \to P, \quad (u,v) \mapsto (u,v,0).
\]

The pull-back metric is
\[
\sigma_0^* g_P = du^2 + \bigl(1 + \alpha(u,v)^2\bigr) dv^2.
\]

Expanding,
\[
\alpha(u,v)^2 = \frac{u^2}{4} + 4u\ell_0 \cos(2v) + 16\ell_0^2 \cos^2(2v).
\]

To eliminate the cross term globally while preserving the ruled character and the \(\sin(2v)\) height modulation, perform the coordinate change on the base:
\[
r(u,v) := u + 8\ell_0 \sin(2v).
\]

This is a global diffeomorphism of \(B\) (Jacobian determinant equals 1 and the map is proper). In the new coordinates \((r,v)\), the induced metric on the image of \(\sigma_0\) becomes exactly
\[
ds^2 = dr^2 + \bigl(r^2 + 16\ell_0^2 \cos^2(2v)\bigr) dv^2,
\]
which is the SAM3 right conoid metric.

\begin{proposition}
The image \(\sigma_0(B)\) equipped with the pulled-back metric is isometric to the SAM3 right conoid. The rulings remain straight lines tangent to \(\partial_r\).
\end{proposition}

\section{Incorporation of the Binary Icosahedral Symmetry}

The binary icosahedral group \(2I \subset \mathrm{SU}(2) \cong S^3\) (order 120) acts on \(S^3\) by left multiplication. This descends to an action on the base \(S^2\) of the classical Hopf fibration. We pull this action back to our cylindrical base \(B\) by identifying the \(S^1_v\) factor with a great circle in \(S^2\) (via Hopf coordinates or stereographic projection).

The explicit homomorphism is
\[
2I \to \mathrm{Diff}(B), \qquad g \cdot (u,v) = (u, \, v + \phi_g),
\]
where \(\phi_g\) is the rotation angle induced on the chosen great circle.

Since \(\cos(2v)\) is invariant under these discrete rotations (they preserve the double-angle periodicity), the connection \(A\) is equivariant and lifts to \(P\).

The \textbf{12 bridges} are the orbits of points with maximal stabilizers under this lifted action. They form smooth horizontal submanifolds with respect to \(A\).

\section{Minimality of the Deformation}

\begin{proposition}[Minimality]
Among all connection 1-forms of the type \(A = d\theta + f(u,v)\, dv\) that are invariant under the above \(2I\)-action and whose curvature contains at most first harmonics in \(v\), the choice
\[
f(u,v) = \frac{u}{2} + 4\ell_0 \cos(2v)
\]
is the unique lowest-degree polynomial deformation that produces a metric on the zero section containing the required \(\cos^2(2v)\) term while preserving the ruled character and exact separability of the Dirac operator.
\end{proposition}
