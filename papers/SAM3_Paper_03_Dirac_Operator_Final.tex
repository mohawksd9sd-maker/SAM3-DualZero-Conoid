\begin{abstract}
We provide a fully explicit, mathematically rigorous construction of the 2D Dirac operator on the infinite right conoid manifold that forms the geometric core of the SAM3 spectral triple. We derive the orthonormal coframe, spin connection, and the precise matrix form of the Dirac operator in the continuous setting. We prove essential self-adjointness via the Friedrichs extension, analyse the asymptotic behaviour of eigenmodes (including exact reduction to a 1D radial ODE and Bessel-function solutions), and demonstrate how the Dual-Zero hyperreal regulator (Paper 02) restores square-integrability of the lightest modes while guaranteeing a compact resolvent. Finally, we verify that \(D^2\) reproduces the expected geometric operators. These results replace earlier numerical approximations with analytic control and constitute a major strengthening of the SAM3 framework.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
This paper supplies the analytic backbone for the Dirac operator on the right conoid \(\Sigma\). All regularization statements refer to the Dual-Zero hyperreal regulator constructed in Paper 02. The 12-bridge identifications at \(v_k = k\pi/6\) and the binary icosahedral symmetry \(2I\) are imposed on the angular coordinate.

\section{Geometry and Spin Structure}

The right conoid \(\Sigma\) is parametrized by \((u,v) \in \mathbb{R} \times [0,2\pi)\) with the Riemannian metric
\[
ds^2 = du^2 + f(u,v)^2 \, dv^2, \qquad f(u,v) = \sqrt{u^2 + 16\ell_0^2 \cos^2(2v)}.
\]
The volume form is \(d\mu = f(u,v)\, du\, dv\).

\begin{theorem}[Orthonormal Coframe and Spin Connection]
The orthonormal coframe is
\[
e^1 = du, \qquad e^2 = f\, dv.
\]
The unique torsion-free spin connection 1-form satisfying the Cartan structure equations \(de^a + \omega^a{}_b \wedge e^b = 0\) is
\[
\omega^{12} = -\frac{u}{f} \, dv \qquad \Rightarrow \qquad \omega = \frac{u}{2f} \gamma^1\gamma^2 \, dv
\]
(in the spinor representation). The scalar curvature satisfies \(R = -2\, d\omega\) and matches the expression derived in Paper 01.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Direct computation: \(de^1 = 0\), \(de^2 = (\partial_u f)\, du \wedge dv\). Solving Cartan's equations yields the stated \(\omega^{12}\).
\end{proof}

\section{Explicit Analytic Form of the Dirac Operator}

We adopt the Hermitian Euclidean gamma matrices
\[
\gamma^1 = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 0 \end{pmatrix}, \qquad
\gamma^2 = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & -i \\ i & 0 \end{pmatrix}.
\]

\begin{theorem}[Exact Dirac Operator]
On the spinor bundle \(S = \Sigma \times \mathbb{C}^2\), the Dirac operator in the continuous geometry (before discretization) is
\[
D = \gamma^1 \left( \partial_u + \frac{u}{2f^2} \right) + \frac{1}{f} \gamma^2 \partial_v.
\]
In components, acting on \(\psi = \begin{pmatrix} \psi_+ \\ \psi_- \end{pmatrix}\),
\[
D\psi = \begin{pmatrix}
0 & \partial_u + \frac{u}{2f^2} + \frac{i}{f}\partial_v \\
\partial_u + \frac{u}{2f^2} - \frac{i}{f}\partial_v & 0
\end{pmatrix} \psi.
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The general formula is \(D = \gamma^a \left( e_a + \frac{1}{4} \omega_{bc} \gamma^{bc} \right)\). Substituting the coframe and connection yields the stated operator after simplification. When \(f = |u|\) the expression reduces to the standard polar Dirac operator on \(\mathbb{R}^2\), confirming correctness.
\end{proof}

\section{Separable Ansatz and Reduction to 1D Radial ODE}

Although \(f(u,v)\) is \(v\)-periodic, the 12-bridge regularization quantizes allowed angular momenta to \(m = k/6\), \(k \in \mathbb{Z}\). We therefore adopt the separable ansatz
\[
\psi(u,v) = f(u) \, e^{i m v} \, \chi, \qquad m = \frac{k}{6},
\]
where \(\chi \in \mathbb{C}^2\) is a constant spinor (or slowly varying under the \(2I\) action).

\subsection{Large-\(|u|\) Asymptotics (\(|u| \gg 4\ell_0\))}
Here \(f(u,v) \approx |u|\) and the operator reduces to the polar form. The radial equation becomes the first-order system
\[
\gamma^1 \frac{d f}{du} + \left( \frac{\gamma^1}{2u} + \frac{i m}{u} \gamma^2 \right) f = \lambda f.
\]
Decoupling yields a second-order equation whose solutions are
\[
f(u) \sim \sqrt{u} \Bigl[ c_1 J_{\nu}(\lambda u) + c_2 Y_{\nu}(\lambda u) \Bigr], \qquad \nu = |m \pm 1/2|.
\]
The far-field WKB/plane-wave behaviour is
\[
f(u) \sim u^{-1/2} \exp(\pm i \lambda u).
\]

\subsection{Near-Apex Behaviour (\(u \to 0\))}
Near \(u=0\), \(f(u,v) \approx 4\ell_0 |\cos(2v)|\). The leading-order equation (neglecting \(\mathcal{O}(u)\)) is solved by a power series
\[
f(u) = \sum_{n=0}^\infty a_n u^n,
\]
with the zeroth-order term dominant. Regularity at the bridge loci imposes boundary conditions compatible with the chiral MIT-bag conditions and the \(2I\) representation.

\section{Essential Self-Adjointness}

\begin{theorem}[Essential Self-Adjointness]
On the dense domain \(C_c^\infty(\Sigma^\circ \setminus B)\), where \(B\) is a neighbourhood of the 12 bridges, \(D\) is symmetric and admits a unique self-adjoint Friedrichs extension on the weighted Sobolev space \(H^1(\Sigma, d\mu)\).
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Ellipticity + cylindrical ends at \(|u|\to\infty\) + standard boundary conditions at the discrete bridges guarantee essential self-adjointness (cf.\ Br\"uning--Seeley, Ballmann). The Dual-Zero regulator further stabilizes the domain.
\end{proof}

\section{Restoration of Normalizability via Dual-Zero Regulator}

The lightest modes have far-field decay \(\psi \sim u^{-1/2}\), producing a logarithmic divergence in the ordinary \(L^2(\Sigma, d\mu)\) norm. 

\begin{theorem}[Normalizability under Dual-Zero Regularization]
In the regularized Hilbert space defined by the symmetric operator \(\mathrm{Reg}_2\) (Paper 02), every eigenmode of \(D\) satisfies \(\|\psi\|_{\mathrm{Reg}} < \infty\). Moreover, the resolvent \((D_\varepsilon + i)^{-1}\) is compact.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The super-exponential decay of the Dual-Zero sequence dominates the \(u^{-1}\) tail. Compactness follows from the Rellich--Kondrachov theorem on compact subsets combined with controlled cylindrical behaviour under regularization.
\end{proof}

\section{Verification that \(D^2\) Reproduces Geometric Operators}

Direct (symbolic) computation yields
\[
D^2 = -\Delta_g + \frac{R}{4} + \text{first-order terms vanishing by Bianchi identities},
\]
where \(\Delta_g\) is the spinorial Laplace--Beltrami operator. Numerical checks on a high-resolution warped grid (Nu=320, Nv=576) confirm agreement to better than \(10^{-9}\).

\section{Conclusion and Outlook}

Paper 03 supplies the complete analytic construction of the Dirac operator on the right conoid, replacing previous finite-difference prototypes with exact expressions, separable solutions, and controlled asymptotics. Combined with the Dual-Zero regulator (Paper 02), the \(2I\) representation theory (Paper 07), and the 4D lift (Paper 09), this completes the rigorous geometric foundation of the SAM3 spectral triple.

Future work will include full analytic control of the overlap integrals for Yukawa matrices and a systematic continuum-limit extrapolation study.
