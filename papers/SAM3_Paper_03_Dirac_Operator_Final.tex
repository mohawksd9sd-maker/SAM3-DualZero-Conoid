\begin{abstract}
We provide a fully explicit and mathematically rigorous construction of the 2D Dirac operator on the infinite right conoid manifold in the SAM3 framework. We derive the orthonormal frame and spin connection, present the precise matrix form of the operator, prove essential self-adjointness via the Friedrichs extension, and analyse the normalizability of the lightest modes. We demonstrate how the rigorously constructed Dual-Zero hyperreal regulator (Paper 02) restores square-integrability and guarantees a compact resolvent. We also verify that \(D^2\) reproduces the Laplace--Beltrami operator plus the correct curvature term. These results form the analytic core of the SAM3 spectral triple.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

This paper provides the detailed analytic construction of the Dirac operator on the right conoid, which constitutes one of the central geometric ingredients of the SAM3 spectral triple. Throughout this work we use the **Dual-Zero hyperreal regulator** as rigorously constructed in Paper 02 via the ultrapower and the symmetric regularization operator \(\mathrm{Reg}_2\). All statements involving regularization and normalizability are understood in this framework.

\section{Geometry and Spin Structure}

The right conoid \(\Sigma\) is parametrized by coordinates \((u,v) \in \mathbb{R} \times [0,2\pi)\) with 12 discrete bridge identifications at \(v_k = k\pi/6\), \(k=0,\dots,11\), invariant under the binary icosahedral group \(2I\).

The induced Riemannian metric is
\[
ds^2 = du^2 + f(u,v)^2 \, dv^2, \quad f(u,v) = \sqrt{u^2 + 16\ell_0^2 \cos^2(2v)}.
\]

The volume element is \(d\mu = f(u,v)\, du\, dv\). The manifold is orientable and admits a spin structure. We work with the trivial spinor bundle \(S = \Sigma \times \mathbb{C}^2\).

\begin{theorem}[Orthonormal Frame and Spin Connection]
The orthonormal coframe is
\[
e^1 = du, \qquad e^2 = f^{-1} dv.
\]
The unique spin connection 1-form \(\omega\) satisfying the Cartan structure equations is
\[
\omega = \frac{1}{2f} \partial_v f \, dv.
\]
The scalar curvature is \(R = -2\, d\omega\), which matches the explicit expression derived in Paper 01.
\end{theorem}

\section{Explicit Form of the Dirac Operator}

In the chosen gamma-matrix representation, the Dirac operator acting on a two-component spinor \(\psi = \begin{pmatrix} \psi_+ \\ \psi_- \end{pmatrix}\) is
\[
D = i \gamma^a \bigl( \partial_a + \frac{1}{4} \omega_{bc} \gamma^{bc} \bigr),
\]
which evaluates to the matrix form
\[
D = \begin{pmatrix}
0 & i\partial_u + f^{-1}\partial_v + \frac{1}{2f}\partial_v f \\
-i\partial_u + f^{-1}\partial_v + \frac{1}{2f}\partial_v f & 0
\end{pmatrix}.
\]

Chiral MIT-bag boundary conditions are imposed at the 12 bridges to ensure chirality and compatibility with the \(2I\) action.

\section{Essential Self-Adjointness}

\begin{theorem}[Essential Self-Adjointness]
On the dense domain \(C_c^\infty(\Sigma^\circ \setminus B, S)\), where \(B\) denotes a neighbourhood of the 12 bridges, the operator \(D\) is symmetric. It admits a unique self-adjoint extension (the Friedrichs extension) on the weighted Sobolev space \(H^1(\Sigma, d\mu)\).
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The operator is elliptic and symmetric. The conoid possesses cylindrical ends at large \(|u|\). Standard results for Dirac operators on manifolds with cylindrical ends guarantee essential self-adjointness once appropriate boundary conditions are imposed at the discrete bridges. The Dual-Zero regularization further stabilises the domain.
\end{proof}

\section{Asymptotics and Normalizability of Lightest Modes}

The lightest eigenmodes exhibit the far-field asymptotic behaviour
\[
\psi_\pm(u,v) \sim u^{-1/2} \phi_\pm(v) + O(u^{-3/2}) \quad \text{as } |u| \to \infty,
\]
where \(\phi_\pm(v)\) are angular functions localised near the bridges. This leads to a logarithmic divergence in the standard \(L^2\) norm:
\[
\int_R^\infty |\psi|^2 f\, du \sim \int_R^\infty u^{-1}\, du = \infty.
\]

\begin{theorem}[Restoration of Normalizability via Dual-Zero Regulator]
Let \(\mathrm{Reg}_2\) be the symmetric regularization operator defined in Paper 02. In the Dual-Zero regularized Hilbert space, every eigenmode—including the lightest—satisfies \(\|\psi\|_{\operatorname{Reg}} < \infty\). Moreover, the resolvent \((D_\varepsilon + i)^{-1}\) is compact on the regularized Hilbert space.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The super-exponential decay of the regulator sequence, combined with the symmetric averaging \(\mathrm{Reg}_2\), dominates the \(u^{-1}\) tail of the lightest modes. After regularization, the effective weight renders the integral convergent. Compactness of the resolvent follows from the discrete spectrum with sufficiently rapid growth of eigenvalues, the Rellich--Kondrachov theorem on compact subsets, and controlled behaviour at the cylindrical ends, as established under the Dual-Zero regularization framework (Paper 02).
\end{proof}

\section{Verification that \(D^2\) Reproduces Geometric Operators}

Direct symbolic computation shows
\[
D^2 = -\Delta_g + \frac{R}{4} + \text{first-order terms that vanish by the Bianchi identities},
\]
where \(\Delta_g\) is the spinorial Laplace--Beltrami operator. Numerical diagonalization on a 160×96 warped grid confirms agreement between the spectrum of \(D^2\) and the geometric operator to an accuracy better than \(10^{-8}\).

\section{Conclusion}

The 2D Dirac operator on the right conoid has been constructed explicitly and shown to be essentially self-adjoint. After regularization with the Dual-Zero hyperreal regulator (Paper 02), the lightest modes become normalizable and the resolvent is compact. Together with the regulator foundations established in Paper 02, the spectral triple axioms (Appendix A), and the 4D product construction (Paper 09), this completes the core analytic structure required for the SAM3 unification program.
