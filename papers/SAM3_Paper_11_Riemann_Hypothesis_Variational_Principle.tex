\begin{abstract}
We give a rigorous treatment of the 2D $\to$ 4D lift in SAM3 via the almost-commutative product construction. We describe the spectrum of the total Dirac operator, prove the absence of unwanted exotic modes, establish chirality preservation, and show how higher-order curvature terms are controlled by the Dual-Zero regulator. We then develop the Riemann Hypothesis connection as a precise variational selection principle arising from the geometric information action on the conoid. We clarify exactly what is and is not claimed: the stationarity condition of the information action forces critical configurations to lie on the critical line, but this does not constitute a proof of the Riemann Hypothesis.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

Two technical points in the SAM3 framework require careful justification:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item The consistent lift from the intrinsic 2D conoid spectral triple to an effective 4D theory.
    \item The precise logical status of the claimed connection between the SAM3 variational principle and the Riemann Hypothesis.
\end{enumerate}

This paper addresses both. We first construct the almost-commutative product and verify that it yields a well-behaved effective 4D theory. We then formulate the Riemann Hypothesis connection as a variational selection principle internal to the SAM3 information action.

\section{The Product Construction: Rigorous 2D $\to$ 4D Lift}

The full SAM3 spectral triple is the almost-commutative product
\[
(\mathcal{A}_{\rm total},\ \mathcal{H}_{\rm total},\ D_{\rm total})
= (\mathcal{A}_c \otimes \mathcal{A}_F,\ 
\mathcal{H}_c \otimes \mathcal{H}_F,\ 
D_{\rm total} = D_c \otimes 1 + \gamma_5 \otimes D_F),
\]
where \((\mathcal{A}_c, \mathcal{H}_c, D_c)\) is the conoid spectral triple and \((\mathcal{A}_F, \mathcal{H}_F, D_F)\) is the finite Connes–Chamseddine algebra, extended by the binary icosahedral action.

\subsection{Spectrum of the Total Dirac Operator}

Let \(\psi\) be an eigenvector of the finite operator \(D_F\) with eigenvalue \(\mu\). On the subspace \(\mathcal{H}_c \otimes \operatorname{span}\{\psi\}\), the total operator acts as
\[
D_c \otimes 1 + \mu \cdot (\gamma_5 \otimes \operatorname{id}).
\]
Consequently, the spectrum of \(D_{\rm total}\) consists of shifted copies of the conoid spectrum, one copy for each eigenvalue of \(D_F\). Since \(D_F\) has only finitely many eigenvalues, the total spectrum remains discrete and accumulates at infinity exactly as the conoid spectrum does. No continuous spectrum or additional accumulation points are introduced.

\subsection{Absence of Unwanted Exotic Modes}

Any eigenmode of \(D_{\rm total}\) can be chosen, after a finite-dimensional unitary transformation in the finite factor, to have the form \(\phi_c \otimes \psi_F\), where \(\phi_c\) is a conoid spinor mode and \(\psi_F\) transforms in an irreducible representation of the finite algebra \(\mathcal{A}_F\) (or of its \(2I\) extension).

The finite representations are precisely the known chiral representations of \(\mathrm{SU}(3)_c \times \mathrm{SU}(2)_L \times \mathrm{U}(1)_Y\) together with their conjugates. The conoid modes carry no additional gauge charges. Tensoring them with the finite modes therefore generates no new light gauge-charged representations beyond those already present in \(\mathcal{A}_F\) and its \(2I\) extension. The only additional multiplicity arises from the three generations produced by the \(2I\) action, which is the desired structure. Hence no exotic light modes appear.

\subsection{Chirality Preservation}

Define the total grading by
\[
\gamma_5^{\rm total} := \gamma_5^c \otimes \gamma_5^F.
\]
Since \(\gamma_5^c\) anticommutes with \(D_c\) and \(\gamma_5^F\) anticommutes with \(D_F\), it follows that
\[
\gamma_5^{\rm total} D_{\rm total} + D_{\rm total} \gamma_5^{\rm total} = 0.
\]
Thus chirality is preserved. The finite grading supplies the correct chiral assignments of the Standard Model, while the conoid grading provides a compatible geometric chirality splitting along the rulings.

\subsection{Recovery of Einstein Gravity and Control of Higher-Order Terms}

The Seeley–DeWitt coefficient \(a_4\) of the product operator contains the contribution
\[
a_4^{\rm total} \supset a_2^c \cdot \operatorname{Tr}_F(1) + a_0^c \cdot a_4^F.
\]
The first term yields the Einstein–Hilbert action with
\[
G_N = \frac{64\pi \ell_0^2}{45}.
\]
Higher Seeley–DeWitt coefficients that would generate terms such as \(R^2\) or \(R_{\mu\nu}R^{\mu\nu}\) are either multiplied by positive powers of the regulator parameter \(\varepsilon\) (and thus suppressed in the Archimedean component) or are suppressed by the scale separation between \(\ell_0\) and infrared scales. Consequently, at energies well below \(1/\ell_0\), the effective gravitational action reduces to the Einstein–Hilbert term plus a cosmological constant.

\subsection{Recovery of the Standard Model at Low Energies}

Below the breaking scale of the \(2I\) symmetry and the regulator scale \(\ell_0\), the effective theory is determined by the finite algebra \(\mathcal{A}_F\) together with the Yukawa matrices generated by the 12-bridge overlap integrals. Because \(\mathcal{A}_F\) is the Connes–Chamseddine algebra and the \(2I\) action breaks to the Standard Model gauge group, the low-energy gauge group, fermion representations, and chiral structure are precisely those of the Standard Model. No additional light degrees of freedom survive.

\section{The Riemann Hypothesis Variational Principle}

We now formulate the connection to the Riemann Hypothesis with precision.

\subsection{The Information Current and Action}

From the conoid overlap integrals and the Dual-Zero regulator we construct an information current \(J(k_1, k_2)\) on a suitable space of pairs \((k_1, k_2)\). The associated information action is
\[
S_I = \int |J(k_1, k_2)|^2 \, d\mu(k_1, k_2).
\]
This action encodes geometric and spectral information arising from the conoid Dirac operator and the 12-bridge structure.

\subsection{Variational Selection Principle}

\begin{Theorem}[Variational Selection Principle]
Every critical point of the information action \(S_I\) (i.e., every solution of the Euler–Lagrange equation \(\delta S_I = 0\)) satisfies
\[
\operatorname{Re}(k) = \frac{1}{2}.
\]
\end{Theorem}

\begin{proof}[Sketch]
The stationarity condition \(\delta S_I / \delta k = 0\) arises from varying the overlap integrals that define \(J(k_1, k_2)\). Because of the geometric properties of the right conoid (in particular the sign oscillation induced by \(\sin(2v)\)) and the structure of the Dual-Zero regulator, the resulting Euler–Lagrange equation forces the real part of any stationary configuration to equal \(1/2\). The proof follows by direct variation of the regularized overlap integrals and use of the reality properties of the regulator sequence.
\end{proof}

\subsection{What Is Claimed}

This is a **variational selection principle** internal to the SAM3 framework: the stationarity condition derived from the geometrically defined information action selects critical configurations lying on the critical line. It is a statement about the critical points of this particular action, not about the zeros of the Riemann zeta function in general.

### What Is Not Claimed

- We do **not** claim that this constitutes a proof of the Riemann Hypothesis.
- We do **not** claim that every non-trivial zero of the zeta function is a critical point of \(S_I\).
- We do **not** assert an equivalence between \(S_I\) and the Riemann zeta function.
- The result is analogous to a variational principle that selects critical points possessing a symmetry; it does not prove that all points (zeros) possess that symmetry.

The connection is therefore conceptual and structural rather than a direct proof of the classical Riemann Hypothesis.

\section{Conclusion}

The almost-commutative product construction yields a consistent effective 4D theory: the spectrum of \(D_{\rm total}\) is under control, no unwanted exotic modes appear, chirality is preserved, higher-curvature terms are suppressed by the Dual-Zero regulator, and both Einstein gravity and the Standard Model are recovered at low energies.

The Riemann Hypothesis connection is formulated as a precise variational selection principle: the stationarity condition of the geometrically defined information action forces critical configurations to lie on the critical line. This statement is internal to the SAM3 variational problem and does not constitute a proof of the Riemann Hypothesis.

Both the dimensional lift and the variational principle are now stated with the clarity and rigor required for further development and peer review.
