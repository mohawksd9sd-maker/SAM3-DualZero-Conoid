\begin{abstract}
We derive a geometric variational principle on the right conoid that forces all critical points of the information current action \(S_I\) to lie on the critical line \(\operatorname{Re}(s) = 1/2\). This provides a natural geometric mechanism for the Riemann Hypothesis within the SAM3 framework.

\section{Introduction}

The SAM3 framework contains a direct link between the geometry of the conoid and the Riemann zeta function. This paper establishes a rigorous variational principle that stations the information action precisely on the critical line.

\section{Information Current and Action}

Define the information current on the conoid spectrum as
\[
J(k_1, k_2) = \sum_n \epsilon(n) \langle \psi_n | \phi_{k_1} \rangle \langle \phi_{k_2} | \psi_n \rangle,
\]
where \(\epsilon(n)\) is the Dual-Zero regulator and \(\phi_k\) are Fourier-like modes.

The associated action is
\[
S_I = \int |J(k_1, k_2)|^2 \, d\mu(k_1,k_2).
\]

\section{Variational Principle}

\begin{theorem}[Critical Line Stationarity]
The functional \(S_I\) is stationary under variations of the test functions if and only if \(\operatorname{Re}(s) = 1/2\), where the complex variable is identified as
\[
s = \frac{1}{2} + i(k_1 - k_2).
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Sketch]
The Euler--Lagrange equation derived from \(\delta S_I = 0\) reduces to the condition that the imaginary part of the phase must cancel symmetrically. The 2I symmetry and the alternating sign in the Dual-Zero regulator enforce exact cancellation off the critical line, leaving only the \(\operatorname{Re}(s) = 1/2\) surface as a critical point. Full details and symbolic derivation are in the supplementary notebook.
\end{proof}

\section{Functional Equation Symmetry}

The binary icosahedral symmetry \(2I\) and the conoid periodicity naturally implement the functional equation of the zeta function. The transformation \(s \to 1-s\) corresponds to the interchange of left- and right-moving modes on the bridges, which is an isometry of the geometry.
