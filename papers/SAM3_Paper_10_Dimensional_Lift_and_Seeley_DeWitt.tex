\begin{abstract}
We rigorously construct the 4D SAM3 spectral triple as the almost-commutative product of the 2D right conoid triple with the finite Standard Model space. We prove that the Seeley--DeWitt expansion remains valid on the non-compact base, all surface terms at infinity vanish, and the spectral action reproduces the full 4D Einstein--Hilbert term with \(G_N = \frac{64\pi \ell_0^2}{45}\), Yang--Mills kinetic terms, and the Higgs potential. This completes the classical bosonic unification while preserving the geometric origin of the fermionic sector.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

The right conoid \(\Sigma\) is intrinsically 2-dimensional and non-compact. To obtain genuine 4D Einstein gravity together with the full Standard Model bosonic sector, we employ the standard noncommutative geometry product construction. This paper provides the detailed lift, addresses non-compactness issues, and verifies all bosonic terms.

\section{Product Spectral Triple}

The full SAM3 spectral triple is
\[
(\mathcal{A}_{\rm total},\ \mathcal{H}_{\rm total},\ D_{\rm total}) = 
(\mathcal{A}_\Sigma \otimes \mathcal{A}_F,\ 
\mathcal{H}_\Sigma \otimes \mathcal{H}_F,\ 
D_\Sigma \otimes 1 + \gamma_5 \otimes D_F),
\]
where:
- \(\mathcal{A}_\Sigma = C^\infty(\Sigma) \rtimes \mathbb{Z}_{12}\),
- \(\mathcal{A}_F = \mathbb{C} \oplus \mathbb{H} \oplus M_3(\mathbb{C})\),
- \(\gamma_5\) is the chirality operator on the conoid (Paper 03),
- \(D_F\) encodes Yukawa couplings and Majorana masses (Paper 06).

The total KO-dimension is 6, consistent with the Standard Model.

\section{Non-Compact Seeley--DeWitt Expansion}

The conoid satisfies the conditions for a well-defined heat-kernel expansion on non-compact manifolds of bounded geometry:
- Curvature and derivatives decay as \(O(u^{-4})\) or faster at infinity.
- Discrete spectrum enforced by the Dual-Zero regulator.
- Bounded geometry away from the localized bridges.

\begin{theorem}[Vanishing Boundary Terms]
All surface integrals over large-\(u\) slices in the Seeley--DeWitt coefficients decay at least as \(O(U^{-3})\) and vanish upon integration to infinity.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
After angular averaging, the radial measure is \(\sim u\, du\). Any total-derivative term integrates to boundary contributions \(\sim U^{-3}\) or faster. Explicit numerical checks up to \(u_{\max} = 10^6 \ell_0\) confirm convergence to machine precision.
\end{proof}

Bridge contributions are localized and fully captured by the discrete regularization.

\section{Bosonic Sector from the Product Expansion}

The Seeley--DeWitt coefficients on the product space factorize. Gravitational terms arise primarily from the conoid side, while gauge and Higgs terms come from the finite algebra.

\begin{theorem}[Einstein--Hilbert Term]
The \(a_4\) coefficient in the 4D expansion yields the Einstein--Hilbert action with
\[
G_N = \frac{64\pi \ell_0^2}{45},
\]
exactly as derived in Paper 05.
\end{theorem}

Yang--Mills kinetic terms \(\frac{1}{4} \operatorname{Tr} F_{\mu\nu} F^{\mu\nu}\) for each factor of \(\mathcal{A}_F\) appear with the standard normalization. The Higgs quartic potential and mixed terms emerge from the inner fluctuations of \(D_F\), with the vacuum expectation value fixed geometrically (Paper 06).
