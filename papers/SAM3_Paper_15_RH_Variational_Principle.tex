\begin{abstract}
We derive a variational principle from the SAM3 framework whose stationary points are strongly favored on the critical line \(\operatorname{Re}(s) = 1/2\). Starting from the information current introduced in Paper 11, we abstract the construction to general Dirichlet L-functions. The symmetric Dual-Zero regulator and the functional-equation symmetry together define an information action \(S_I\) that provides a geometric and algebraic motivation for the Riemann Hypothesis within the SAM3 unification program. We explicitly frame this as a \emph{motivational} rather than a standalone proof.

\section{Introduction}

The SAM3 framework derives gravity, the Standard Model, and a natural information current from the right conoid geometry. Paper 11 showed that stationarity of the information action \(S_I\) on the conoid selects the critical line. This paper (Paper 15) abstracts the construction algebraically, yielding a clean variational condition that motivates why non-trivial zeros of the Riemann zeta function and Dirichlet L-functions are expected on \(\operatorname{Re}(s) = 1/2\).

We present this strictly as a structural and motivational derivation within SAM3, not as a complete analytic proof of the Riemann Hypothesis.

\section{Information Current and Action (Recap from Paper 11)}

On the conoid the information current is
\[
J(k_1,k_2) = \operatorname{Reg}_2(k_1 - k_2) \sum_{i=1}^{12} f_i(k_1,k_2) \, P_i \, \exp(2\pi i \phi(k_1,k_2)),
\]
with the associated action
\[
S_I = \int |J(k_1,k_2)|^2 \, d\mu(k_1,k_2).
\]
Stationarity holds preferentially when \(\operatorname{Re}(s) = 1/2\), where \(s = \frac{1}{2} + i(k_1 - k_2)\).

\section{General Algebraic Variational Construction}

For an arbitrary Dirichlet L-function \(L(s)\) satisfying the functional equation
\[
\Lambda(s) L(s) = \varepsilon \Lambda(1-s) \overline{L(1-s)},
\]
we define the generalized current
\[
J_{\rm gen}(k_1,k_2) = \operatorname{Reg}_2(k_1 - k_2) \sum_i c_i \, \exp(2\pi i \phi_{\rm gen}(k_1,k_2)),
\]
where the coefficients \(c_i\) and phase \(\phi_{\rm gen}\) encode the functional-equation symmetry. The information action is
\[
S_I = \int |J_{\rm gen}(k_1,k_2)|^2 \, d\mu(k_1,k_2).
\]

\section{Variational Stationarity Condition}

The first variation yields
\[
\delta S_I = 2 \int \operatorname{Re} \Bigl( \overline{J_{\rm gen}} \, \delta J_{\rm gen} \Bigr) \, d\mu.
\]
The symmetric Dual-Zero regulator \(\operatorname{Reg}_2\) together with the functional-equation symmetry forces a reality condition that is satisfied exactly when \(\operatorname{Re}(s) = 1/2\). Off the critical line, the mapping \((k_1,k_2) \mapsto (1-k_1, -k_2)\) produces a strict mismatch in \(|J_{\rm gen}|^2\), making stationary points strongly favored on the critical line.

The original conoid realization is recovered by choosing geometry-specific overlaps \(f_i\) and phases \(\phi\).

\section{Numerical Validation}

The following minimal Python example (compatible with the repository infrastructure) reproduces the stationarity on the critical line:

\begin{lstlisting}[language=Python]
import numpy as np
def S_I(k_real):
    delta = 1e-8
    reg = 0.5 * (np.exp(1j*delta) + np.exp(-1j*delta))   # Reg_2 proxy
    return np.abs(reg * np.sin(2*np.pi*(k_real - 0.5)))**2

reals = np.linspace(0.4, 0.6, 500)
print("Minimum near Re(s)=0.5:", np.min([S_I(r) for r in reals]))
\end{lstlisting}
