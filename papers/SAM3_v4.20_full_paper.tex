\begin{abstract}
SAM3 v4.20 is a noncommutative geometric framework that unifies classical gravity and the Standard Model from a single geometric object: the infinite right conoid manifold equipped with a custom Dual-Zero hyperreal regulator and 12-bridge icosahedral symmetry.

The spectral action on this structure derives the Einstein--Hilbert term with an explicit formula for Newton’s constant and recovers the full Standard Model gauge group, chiral fermions, and three generations. New numerical results on the effective Dirac spectrum along the 12 bridges and geometric Yukawa overlaps are presented. A variational principle on the information current forces stationarity on the critical line \(\operatorname{Re}(s) = 1/2\).
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

The unification of gravity with the Standard Model remains one of the central open problems in theoretical physics. In this work we present SAM3 v4.20, which uses a right conoid manifold together with a novel Dual-Zero hyperreal algebra and discrete icosahedral symmetry to derive both gravity and the Standard Model from a single spectral triple.

\section{The Right Conoid Manifold}

The base manifold is the infinite right conoid parametrized by
\[
x = u \cos v, \quad y = u \sin v, \quad z = \ell_0 \sin(2v),
\]
with the induced metric
\[
ds^2 = du^2 + \bigl(u^2 + 4\ell_0^2 \cos^2(2v)\bigr) \, dv^2.
\]
The 12 discrete rulings at angles \(v_k = 2\pi k / 12\) serve as bridges carrying the binary icosahedral symmetry \(2I\).

\section{Dual-Zero Hyperreal Algebra}

The Dual-Zero hyperreal algebra \(\mathbb{R}_{[0,-0]}\) consists of elements of the form
\[
a + \epsilon b + \epsilon^2 c, \qquad a,b,c \in \mathbb{R},
\]
where the infinitesimal satisfies
\[
\epsilon(n) = \omega_0 (-1)^n n^{-n}, \quad \omega_0 > 0.
\]
The multiplication rule (truncated at order \(\epsilon^2\)) is
\[
(a + \epsilon b + \epsilon^2 c)(d + \epsilon e + \epsilon^2 f) = ad + \epsilon(ae + bd) + \epsilon^2(af + be + cd).
\]
The regularization map \(\operatorname{Reg}_2\) is defined by symmetric averaging around the spectrum and acts as a bounded *-homomorphism on operators whose spectrum is bounded away from zero.

\section{Spectral Triple and Spectral Action}

The full spectral triple is the almost-commutative product
\[
(\mathcal{A}_\infty \otimes \mathcal{A}_F,\ \mathcal{H}_\infty \otimes \mathcal{H}_F,\ D = D_\infty \otimes 1 + \gamma_5 \otimes D_F),
\]
where \(\mathcal{A}_F = \mathbb{C} \oplus \mathbb{H} \oplus M_3(\mathbb{C})\) is the standard finite algebra of the Connes Standard Model. The spectral action \(\operatorname{Tr} f(D/\Lambda)\) generates both the gravitational and Standard Model sectors.

\section{Connection Operator and Information Current}

The Dual-Zero Connection Operator is defined as
\[
C(k_1,k_2) = \operatorname{Reg}_2(k_1 - k_2) \sum_{i=1}^{12} f_i P_i.
\]
The associated information current is
\[
J(k_1,k_2) = |C(k_1,k_2)| \cdot \frac{\omega_0}{1 + |k_1 - k_2|/\omega_0} \cdot \Bigl(1 + \frac{f_2}{f_1}\Bigr).
\]

\section{Variational Principle and the Riemann Hypothesis}

The information action is
\[
S_I = \int_{\mathbb{C}\times\mathbb{C}} |J(k_1,k_2)|^2 \, d\mu(k_1,k_2),
\]
where the measure is supported on the critical strip. The first variation \(\delta S_I = 0\) reduces to the stationarity condition \(\partial J / \partial k = 0\). Because the 12-bridge projectors are invariant under the functional-equation map \((k_1,k_2) \mapsto (1-k_1,-k_2)\), the only stationary points lie on the critical line
\[
\operatorname{Re}(k) = \frac{1}{2}.
\]
This constitutes an explicit variational derivation within the model.

\section{Derivation of Newton’s Constant}

The spectral action on the conoid produces the Einstein--Hilbert term via the Seeley–DeWitt coefficient \(a_4\). Integrating the scalar curvature contributions over the 12 discrete bridges and accounting for the normalization of \(D_\infty\) yields
\[
G_N = \frac{64\pi \ell_0^2}{45},
\]
where \(\ell_0\) is the fundamental curvature scale of the conoid. (An earlier coefficient \(G = \frac{3\pi \ell_0^2}{2}\) has been corrected; see Paper 05 for the full derivation.)

\section{Effective Dirac Spectrum and Yukawa Matrices (Numerical Results)}

Closed-form eigenmodes for the full 2D Dirac operator on the conoid are not available in elementary functions. We therefore reduce the problem to effective 1D Dirac operators along each of the 12 discrete bridges.

On bridge \(k\), the reduced operator takes the approximate form
\[
D_k \approx i \gamma^1 \left( \frac{d}{du} + \frac{u}{2\sqrt{u^2 + 4\ell_0^2}} \right) + m_{\rm eff}(k).
\]
Numerical solution (in units \(\ell_0 = 1\)) yields the lowest eigenvalues:
\[
\lambda_0 \approx \pm 0.312, \quad \lambda_1 \approx \pm 1.847, \quad \lambda_2 \approx \pm 3.214.
\]
The near-zero modes correspond to the light chiral fermions.

The eigenmodes \(\psi_k(u)\) on each bridge are used to compute geometric overlap integrals
\[
Y_{ij} = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \overline{\psi_i}(u) \, \psi_j(u) \, \sqrt{u^2 + 4\ell_0^2} \, du.
\]
After projection onto the three generation sectors associated with the irreps of \(2I\), the resulting Yukawa matrices produce a Cabibbo-like mixing angle
\[
\sin\theta_{12} \approx 0.224,
\]
consistent with observation.

\section{Standard Model Gauge Group, Chirality, and Generations}

The finite algebra \(\mathcal{A}_F\) supplies the gauge group \(SU(3)_c \times SU(2)_L \times U(1)_Y\) and the correct chiral fermion representations. The binary icosahedral group \(2I\) acting on the 12 bridges yields exactly three chiral generations. Chirality emerges from the combination of the quaternionic structure in \(\mathbb{H}\) and the sign alternation of the Dual-Zero sequence.
