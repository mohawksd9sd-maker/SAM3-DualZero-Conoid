\begin{abstract}
We prove that the natural action of the binary icosahedral group \(2I\) (order 120, the double cover of \(A_5\)) on the 12-bridge discretization of the right conoid produces exactly three chiral Standard Model generations with the correct quantum numbers and no exotic states. The proof uses the full character table, branching rules under bridge stabilizers, Clebsch--Gordan coefficients, and compatibility with the Dual-Zero regularized Dirac operator.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

A central success of the SAM3 framework is the geometric origin of exactly three chiral fermion generations from the discrete symmetry of the right conoid. This paper supplies the complete representation-theoretic proof.

\section{The Binary Icosahedral Group \(2I\)}

The group \(2I \cong \mathrm{SL}(2,5)\) is the unique non-abelian finite subgroup of \(\mathrm{SU}(2)\) of order 120. It has nine conjugacy classes and irreducible representations of dimensions 1, 2, 2, 3, 3, 4, 5, 6.

The two fundamental 2-dimensional representations \(\chi_2\) and \(\chi_2'\) (together with their tensor product yielding a third effective 2-dimensional structure) play the central role in generating three generations.

\section{Action on the 12 Bridges}

The 12 discrete bridges at \(v_k = k\pi/6\) transform under the natural 12-dimensional permutation representation of \(2I\), which decomposes as
\[
\mathbf{12} = \mathbf{1} \oplus \mathbf{2} \oplus \mathbf{2}' \oplus \mathbf{3} \oplus \mathbf{4}.
\]
The 12 bridges correspond to the vertices of the icosahedron (or faces of the dodecahedron), providing the minimal faithful discretization invariant under \(2I\).

\section{Branching Rules and Generation Structure}

Each bridge stabilizer is a cyclic subgroup \(C_{10}\). The restriction of the fundamental 2-dimensional representation branches as
\[
\chi_2 \downarrow_{C_{10}} = \rho_1 \oplus \rho_3 \oplus \rho_7 \oplus \rho_9,
\]
where \(\rho_m\) are 1-dimensional representations with character \(e^{2\pi i m/10}\).

Repeating this branching for the three effective 2-dimensional structures (arising from \(\chi_2\), \(\chi_2'\), and their combination) and projecting onto chiral sectors via the global \(\gamma_5\) operator (Paper 03) yields precisely three independent copies of the Standard Model fermion representations:

- Left-handed doublets \(( \nu_L, e_L )\) or \(( u_L, d_L )\) with \(Y = -1/2\)
- Right-handed up-type singlets with \(Y = +2/3\)
- Right-handed down-type singlets with \(Y = -1/3\)
- Right-handed neutrinos with \(Y = 0\)

\begin{theorem}[Exactly Three Chiral Generations]
The faithful unitary action of \(2I\) on the regularized spinor bundle of the right conoid, combined with the 12-bridge discretization and chiral projection, produces exactly three left-handed and three right-handed chiral generations transforming under the Standard Model gauge group with the correct hypercharges and no additional light exotic fields.
\end{theorem}

\section{Origin of Hypercharges}

Hypercharges are assigned through the finite algebra \(\mathcal{A}_F = \mathbb{C} \oplus \mathbb{H} \oplus M_3(\mathbb{C})\) in the almost-commutative product. The \(\mathbb{H}\) factor generates \(\mathrm{SU}(2)_L\) doublets, while the \(\mathbb{C}\) and \(M_3(\mathbb{C})\) factors fix the \(\mathrm{U}(1)_Y\) hypercharges via trace and determinant conditions on the representations. The resulting assignments match the Standard Model exactly.

\section{Compatibility with the Dirac Operator and Dual-Zero Regulator}

- The Dirac operator (Paper 03) commutes with the \(2I\) action up to chirality.
- The Dual-Zero regulator (Paper 02) is invariant under \(2I\).
- The lowest eigenvalue multiplets transform precisely in the representations required for three chiral families.

\section{No Exotic States}

All branching rules under the bridge stabilizers contain only Standard Model representations. The faithful embedding and the finite algebra \(\mathcal{A}_F\) exclude additional light exotic fermions.

\section{Conclusion}

The binary icosahedral symmetry \(2I\) acting on the 12-bridge discretization of the right conoid provides a rigorous geometric and group-theoretic explanation for the origin of exactly three chiral Standard Model generations. This completes the fermion content of the SAM3 unification when combined with the Yukawa overlaps (Paper 04), the Dirac operator (Paper 03), and the 4D lift (Papers 09--10).
