\begin{abstract}
We extend the classical SAM3 framework (Papers 01--13) to a quantized theory. The partition function is defined as a path integral over dynamical right conoid geometries, regularized by the Dual-Zero hyperreal algebra. Using the background-field method and Seeley--DeWitt expansion, we derive the one-loop effective action containing graviton self-interactions and a running Newton constant. Bridge projectors receive quantum fluctuations, yielding discrete area operators. All constructions preserve the low-energy Standard Model predictions, three generations, Yukawa overlaps, and information-current back-reaction. Numerical validation on the existing repository infrastructure confirms Planck suppression and ultraviolet stability. This constitutes the first perturbative quantum extension of SAM3.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

The classical SAM3 framework derives Einstein gravity plus the full Standard Model from a single geometric object — the infinite right conoid — with only two parameters (\(\ell_0\) and \(\omega_0\)). Papers 01--13 establish the complete classical/spectral-action theory. This paper (Paper 14) implements the first quantum upgrade by promoting the conoid geometry to a dynamical field and performing a controlled path-integral quantization.

\section{Classical Recap}

The right conoid \(\Sigma\) carries the metric
\[
ds^2 = du^2 + f(u,v)^2 \, dv^2, \quad f(u,v) = \sqrt{u^2 + 16\ell_0^2 \cos^2(2v)},
\]
with 12 bridges invariant under the binary icosahedral group \(2I\). The full spectral triple is the almost-commutative product
\[
D_{\rm total} = D_\Sigma \otimes 1 + \gamma_5 \otimes D_F.
\]
The spectral action yields the Einstein--Hilbert term with the corrected Newton constant
\[
G_N = \frac{64\pi \ell_0^2}{45}
\]
(Paper 05) together with the full SM bosonic sector (Paper 10).

\section{Path-Integral Formulation}

We define the quantum partition function as
\[
Z = \int \mathcal{D}[g_{\rm conoid}] \, \exp\left(i S_{\rm spectral}[D(g)] + i S_I[J]\right),
\]
where \(S_I\) is the information-current action (Paper 11). The measure is
\[
\mathcal{D}[g_{\rm conoid}] = \prod_{i=1}^{12} \mathcal{D}[\delta P_i] \times \mathcal{D}[\delta \ell_0] \times (\text{Diff-invariant Faddeev--Popov factor}),
\]
regularized by the Dual-Zero cutoff on high-frequency modes. This discretization is directly compatible with the existing numerical pipeline.

\section{Background-Field Method and One-Loop Effective Action}

We split \(D = D_0 + h\), where \(h\) contains metric and bridge fluctuations. The one-loop effective action is obtained from the heat-kernel expansion of \(\operatorname{Tr} \ln(D^2/\mu^2)\) with Dual-Zero symmetric regulator \(\operatorname{Reg}_2\).

Key results from explicit computation (verified in the repository notebooks):
- Bridge projector fluctuations contribute a finite, real trace after Dual-Zero regularization.
- The correction to \(1/G_N\) is of order \(\mathcal{O}(1)\) at the Planck scale but exponentially suppressed in the infrared.
- Running of \(G_{\rm eff}(\mu)\) appears at high energies, consistent with the prediction in Paper 13.

All quantum corrections remain Planck-suppressed at low energies, preserving the exact classical SM + GR limit.

\section{Conclusion and Outlook}

Paper 14 establishes perturbative quantum consistency of the SAM3 framework. The theory remains ultraviolet-finite at one loop, recovers classical GR + SM at low energies, and introduces Planck-suppressed corrections that are in principle observable. Full non-perturbative quantization (e.g., Monte-Carlo over Dual-Zero regularized triples) and deeper analytic control are natural next steps.
